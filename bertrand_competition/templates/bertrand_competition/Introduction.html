In Bertrand Competition, participants play as firm owners(in duopoly market), each deciding simultaneously on how much price to set for their products. The participant with the lowest price carries the day and becomes the winner.

The minimum price to set is {{ minimum_price }}. The maximum price to set is {{ maximum_price }}.

The winner's payoff is calculated as the lowest price set minus the minimum price(taken as marginal costs). The loser has nothing to gain, hence no payoff. In case of a tie, then the payoff is the average of price set by the two participants minus minimum price.
